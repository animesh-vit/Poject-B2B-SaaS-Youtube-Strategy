## Post 1

**Date:** Approx. June 24, 2026

**Main topic:**  
SaaS pricing, positioning, and founder operating lessons from a TinySeed batch.

**Key insights:**

- Around 80% of founders in TinySeed batches have a pricing issue.
- Pricing problems include being too low, using the wrong value metric, or making plans too complicated.
- Pricing is framed as the biggest SaaS growth lever, with positioning as the second.
- Testing whether the lowest pricing tier should be hidden can reveal if it is hurting revenue.
- AI SEO is becoming a real acquisition channel worth watching.
- Spending time with other founders at a similar stage is highly valuable.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS strategy:**

- Audit pricing before chasing more acquisition.
- Review value metrics, plan complexity, and whether the lowest tier attracts the wrong customers.
- Treat positioning as a major growth lever, not just a messaging exercise.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approx. June 17, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Trial funnel friction and conversion strategy for bootstrapped SaaS.

**Key insights:**

- Adding friction to a trial funnel can improve or hurt conversions depending on the product and customer.
- The right amount of friction depends on buyer intent, product complexity, and signup context.
- Different SaaS products can require different funnel designs, even under the same founder.
- Low-volume products still need testing, but founders must combine data with product instincts.
- Product instincts improve through repeated exposure to customer behavior and tradeoff decisions.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS strategy:**

- Do not blindly remove all signup friction.
- Match trial flow to the buyer's intent and the product's complexity.
- Test changes where possible, but use customer understanding when traffic volume is too low for clean experiments.
