## Post 1

**Date:** June 22, 2026  

**Main topic:**  
B2B buyer trust has shifted from owned websites and funnel-based journeys to third-party discovery surfaces such as Reddit, G2, YouTube, independent communities, and AI tools.

**Key insights:**

- Buyers often form their shortlist before booking a demo, using third-party proof points instead of only vendor websites.
- Reddit, review sites, YouTube videos, and AI-generated answers now influence category perception and vendor selection.
- AI tools pull from the same public and third-party sources buyers already use, so off-site visibility affects both human discovery and LLM recommendations.
- Old YouTube content can become valuable again when optimized for buyer questions, search intent, and AI citation.
- Negative reviews should be treated as product and customer experience research, not just reputation issues.
- Niche YouTubers can create bottom-of-funnel content that helps a brand own high-intent SERP and category conversations.
- Brands need to show up in the places where their category is debated, answer real questions, and make their content easy to quote, cite, and trust.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS YouTube strategy:**

- Treat YouTube as a buyer-decision and AI-visibility channel, not just a place to archive webinars.
- Audit existing videos for bottom-of-funnel value: comparison queries, product teardowns, use-case demos, objection handling, and category education.
- Refresh titles, descriptions, chapters, transcripts, and metadata so each video clearly answers a buyer question and can be understood by search engines and AI systems.
- Create content with niche industry YouTubers who already have trust with the target buyer, especially for competitor comparisons, implementation walkthroughs, and practical product evaluations.
- Use themes from Reddit threads, review sites, sales calls, and customer support issues to decide which YouTube videos to create or update.
- Make videos easy to cite by including clear claims, specific examples, customer pains, feature explanations, and concise summaries in the description.

## Post 2

**Date:** June 17, 2026  

**Main topic:**  
YouTube is an underused search and buyer-intent opportunity for B2B SaaS brands, especially across demo, review, comparison, and category keywords.

**Key insights:**

- Foundation's YouTube SERP analysis across more than 8,000 B2B SaaS keywords found YouTube appearing frequently for high-intent searches.
- YouTube appeared in 80% of demo searches, 22% of "best [category]" searches, 20% of "[brand] reviews" searches, and 20% of "[brand] vs [brand]" searches.
- SaaS brands can unlock value by optimizing older YouTube videos with relevant keywords, stronger thumbnails, and better descriptions.
- New YouTube content should be created around priority search intent, not just flashy production.
- Optimization can dramatically expand visibility: one Foundation client reportedly grew from ranking videos for 150 keywords to more than 15,000.
- Building a focused video series around high-value queries can help brands capture demand at scale.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS YouTube strategy:**

- Build a YouTube keyword map around bottom-of-funnel queries: demos, reviews, "best [category]", alternatives, and competitor comparisons.
- Audit the existing video library and update metadata, thumbnails, titles, descriptions, and keyword targeting before creating net-new content.
- Prioritize videos that directly match buyer intent over broad awareness topics.
- Create repeatable video formats for high-value search queries, such as product demos, category explainers, review-style walkthroughs, and comparison videos.
- Track keyword rankings for YouTube videos so optimization work can be measured against search visibility, not only views or subscribers.
