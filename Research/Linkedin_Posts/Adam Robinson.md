### Source: Adam Robinson

## Post 1

**Date:** Approx. June 23, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Early customer discovery from an unexpected beta user.

**Key insights:**

- A customer found and bought an unreleased $997/month MoltSets plan before launch.
- The product had strong coverage, but email validity became the key blocker.
- A missing validation feature created a major quality issue before beta was ready.
- The customer's use case revealed demand from SDR teams using AI tools to self-source leads.
- Fast founder response turned a serious product issue into a renewal opportunity.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS strategy:**

- Treat unexpected early users as high-value discovery, even when the experience breaks.
- Ask for the use case before defending the product.
- Validate the core promise before scaling acquisition, especially when data quality is the product.
- Use early failures to sharpen positioning, roadmap priorities, and launch readiness.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approx. June 22, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Bootstrapper lessons on reaching $1M ARR.

**Key insights:**

- Big launches, newsletters, and redesigns may create little measurable impact.
- Product-led growth can be harder than sales-led growth when buyers need education or demos.
- Users are increasingly discerning and leave quickly when edge cases fail.
- Content creation takes more time than many founders expect.
- Usage limits and pricing strategy can determine whether growth is profitable.
- Customer feedback from support tickets can be enough to guide roadmap priorities.
- $83k/month in revenue can still feel tight after costs, hiring, and reinvestment.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS strategy:**

- Do not assume PLG will work if buyers need explanation or proof.
- Use customer support feedback as a roadmap input, not just a service queue.
- Build pricing and plan limits around margin protection as well as revenue growth.
- Focus messaging so prospects understand the value within seconds.
