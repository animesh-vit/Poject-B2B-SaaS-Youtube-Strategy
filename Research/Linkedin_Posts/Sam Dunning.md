## Post 1

**Date:** Approx. June 23, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Scaling owned media, partnerships, outbound, and offer strength for a SaaS SEO/AEO agency.

**Key insights:**

- Owned media is the main focus: website proof, SEO/AEO visibility, and YouTube.
- The website should prove pipeline impact, not vanity metrics.
- YouTube is moving from one weekly video/podcast to two weekly videos.
- LinkedIn ads support retargeting and cold ICP education.
- Partnerships with SaaS-focused audiences can build trust faster.
- Warm outbound can target closed-lost leads, site visitors, and ad-engaged prospects.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS YouTube strategy:**

- Use YouTube as an owned media engine, not just a content archive.
- Publish breakdowns, workflows, case studies, and interviews that prove expertise.
- Pair YouTube with LinkedIn, ads, partnerships, and outbound so videos support pipeline.
- Use case study videos with real founders and marketing leaders as trust assets.

## Post 2

**Date:** Approx. June 22, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Controlling SaaS visibility in LLMs through bottom-funnel SEO/AEO assets.

**Key insights:**

- SaaS brands can influence LLM visibility by creating clear, intent-matched pages.
- Important assets include vertical pages, industry pages, pricing pages, competitor pages, software list posts, and integration pages.
- The goal is to map every high-intent buyer search path and build the best page for it.
- Classic bottom-funnel SEO still matters for AI visibility.
- Off-site trust signals also matter: brand mentions, backlinks, Reddit, and review sites.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS strategy:**

- Build bottom-funnel content around every way buyers evaluate the product.
- Make pages clear enough for both buyers and AI systems to understand positioning.
- Support owned pages with niche mentions, backlinks, Reddit activity, and review-site presence.
